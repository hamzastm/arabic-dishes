Maqluba (Palestine)


Dish History:

Maqluba, meaning "upside-down" in Arabic, is a traditional dish enjoyed across the Palestine, particularly in Palestine, Jordan, and Syria. Its history dates back centuries, evolving as a hearty and flavorful dish made to celebrate gatherings and family occasions. The unique presentation, with its layered ingredients flipped onto a serving plate, makes it visually striking and delicious.


    Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of basmati rice
  • 500 g of chicken or lamb
  • 2 large eggplants, sliced
  • 2 medium potatoes, sliced
  • 1 large tomato, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of ground allspice
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups of water or broth
  • Optional: yogurt or salad for serving

Preparation Method:

  1. Prepare the chicken or lamb: Boil the meat in water with salt, pepper, and optional spices until tender. Reserve the broth for later use.
  2. Prepare the vegetables: Fry the sliced eggplants and potatoes in vegetable oil until golden. Set them aside on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.
  3. Layer the ingredients: In a large pot, place the tomato slices at the bottom. Layer the fried potatoes and eggplants on top. Add the cooked meat, followed by the uncooked rice.
  4. Season and cook: Sprinkle the spices (allspice, cinnamon, turmeric, black pepper, and salt) over the layers. Pour the reserved broth or water over the ingredients until it just covers the rice.
  5. Cook the Maqluba: Cover the pot and cook on medium heat for about 30–40 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and absorbs the liquid.
  6. Flip and serve: Allow the pot to rest for 5–10 minutes after cooking. Place a large serving dish over the pot, then carefully flip it upside-down. Gently lift the pot to reveal the layered dish.

Serving Method:

Serve Maqluba with a side of yogurt or a fresh salad for a complete meal.
