Mujaddara (Lebanon)


Dish History:

Mujaddara is a traditional Lebanese dish made with rice and lentils. This dish is considered one of the popular foods that dates back to ancient times and was prepared in most of the Levant. It symbolizes simple, nutritious food prepared for daily occasions.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown lentils
  • 1 cup rice
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Method:

  1. Cook the Lentils:
    Rinse the lentils well, then boil them in water until tender (about 15-20 minutes). Drain the lentils from the water.
  2. Prepare the Rice:
    In another pot, cook the rice in salted water until itâ€™s partially cooked, then drain it.
  3. Fry the Onions:
    In a skillet, heat the olive oil and add the chopped onions. Stir until the onions turn golden brown.
  4. Mix the Lentils and Rice:
    In a large bowl, combine the cooked lentils with the partially cooked rice, then add cumin, salt, and pepper to taste. Top with the fried onions.

Serving Method:

The dish is served with yogurt or a green salad.
