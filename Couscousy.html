Couscousy (Tunisia)


Dish History:

Couscousy is a traditional dish originating from Tunisian and Moroccan cuisine. It is considered a staple food in North Africa, with a history spanning over a thousand years. It was traditionally prepared with simple local ingredients such as wheat and vegetables, serving as a primary food source for many families.


    Ingredients:

  • 2 cups couscousy
  • 500 g meat (chicken or lamb)
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 1 eggplant, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp coriander
  • 1/4 tsp red chili powder (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Method:

  1. Preparing the Meat and Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil and brown the meat until golden on all sides. Add the chopped onion and spices such as cumin and coriander, stirring until fragrant.
  2. Adding Vegetables: Add the diced tomatoes, carrots, eggplant, and boiled chickpeas to the pot. Pour in water or broth and let the mixture simmer on low heat until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Cooking the Couscousy: In a separate bowl, place the couscous with a pinch of salt. Steam it in a couscous pot (or use a steamer over a boiling pot) for 10-15 minutes. Fluff with a fork and mix in a bit of olive oil.
  4. Serving: Arrange the couscous on a serving plate, top it with the meat and vegetables, and serve with the broth on the side.

Serving Method:

Couscousy is served with a meat and vegetable broth and can be garnished with toasted nuts or chickpeas.
