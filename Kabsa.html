Kabsa (Saudi Arabia)


Dish History:

Kabsa is one of the most famous dishes in Saudi cuisine. It is believed to have originated in ancient times in the Arabian Peninsula and was traditionally prepared by Bedouins in the desert using simple ingredients like rice and meat or chicken. Today, Kabsa is a staple dish served during most special occasions in Saudi Arabia and is considered a main course for family gatherings or celebrations.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken or meat (such as lamb or beef)
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2 chopped onions
  • 2 chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp Kabsa spices (or a blend of turmeric, black pepper, cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 cup raisins for garnish (optional)
  • 1/4 cup roasted nuts for garnish (optional)

Preparation Method:

  1. Preparing the Chicken or Meat: In a large pot, heat the oil and add the chicken or meat pieces. Sear the chicken or meat on all sides until browned. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until golden.
  2. Adding Tomatoes and Spices: Add the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste to the chicken or meat. Mix in the spices like turmeric, cinnamon, black pepper, and bay leaves. Let the mixture simmer on low heat for 10 minutes.
  3. Preparing the Rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water. Add the rice to the pot with the chicken or meat, and mix well. Pour in water (double the amount of rice) and bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Cooking the Kabsa: Once the water starts boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it cook for 30-40 minutes until the rice and meat are fully cooked.
  5. Garnishing: Garnish the Kabsa with toasted raisins or roasted nuts such as almonds and pine nuts, if desired.

Serving Method:

Kabsa is served hot on a large platter and can be accompanied by yogurt or Arabic salad on the side.
