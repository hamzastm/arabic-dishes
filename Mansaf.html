Mansaf (Jordan)


Dish History:

Mansaf is the national dish of Jordan and one of the main dishes served during special occasions such as weddings, festivals, and social gatherings. Its history dates back to ancient times when it was considered a special dish for kings and Bedouin tribes. It was offered to clans as a sign of hospitality and has gained fame in Jordan and the rest of the Levant region.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 kg lamb
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1/2 kg Jameed yogurt
  • 2 chopped onions
  • 2 tbsp ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp Mansaf spices (cinnamon, cardamom, black pepper)
  • Nuts (almonds and pine nuts) for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Preparation Method:

  1. Preparing the Lamb: In a large pot, place the lamb with water, then add the onions and spices (cinnamon, cardamom, black pepper). Boil on low heat for 1.5 to 2 hours until the lamb is fully cooked.
  2. Preparing the Yogurt: Dissolve the Jameed yogurt in hot water until it becomes liquid. Add it to the lamb and cook the mixture on low heat until the yogurt blends well with the broth.
  3. Preparing the Rice: In a pan, heat the ghee or oil and add the rice after washing it well. Cook the rice using the lamb broth until fully cooked. You can add spices like cinnamon or cardamom for extra flavor.
  4. Assembling the Dish: On a large serving platter, place the rice first, then arrange the cooked lamb on top. Cover the dish with the Jameed yogurt.
  5. Garnishing: Garnish the Mansaf with roasted nuts such as pine nuts or almonds.

Serving Method:

Mansaf is served with Arabic bread or can be enjoyed with salted yogurt. It is typically presented during large gatherings as a symbol of generosity and hospitality.
