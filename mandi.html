Mandi (Yemen)


Dish History:

Mandi is a traditional dish that originated from the Hadhramaut region in Yemen.[2] It consists mainly of meat and rice with a blend of spices, and is cooked in a pit. It is consumed in most areas of the Arabian Peninsula and also found in Egypt, the Levant, Turkey, Kerala and Southeast Asia.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken or meat
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp Mandi spices (or a mix of cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, cumin, black pepper)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • Roasted nuts (almonds or pine nuts)
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)

Preparation Method:

  1. Cooking the Meat or Chicken: In a large pot, heat the ghee and add the chicken or meat pieces. Sear them until well-browned. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden.
  2. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water to remove excess starch, In the same pot with the remaining chicken stock (and spices), add the rinsed rice. Add enough water to cook the rice (about 3 cups). Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and cook for about 15-20 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and all the liquid is absorbed.
  3. Serve:Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork and serve it with the whole chicken (or pieces) on top,Optional: Garnish with some fried onions or nuts if you like.

Serving Method:

placing the cooked rice on a platter, topping it with the whole chicken or pieces, and garnishing with fried onions, roasted almonds.It’s often served with yogurt and salad
