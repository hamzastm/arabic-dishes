Tagine (Morocco)


Dish History:

Tagine, named after the distinctive earthenware pot it is cooked in, is a hallmark of Moroccan cuisine. Originating from the Berber people, it has been a staple in Moroccan households for centuries. The slow-cooking method in the tagine pot allows the flavors to meld together beautifully, creating a dish that is rich, aromatic, and layered with spices.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, lamb, or beef
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cups of vegetables (e.g., carrots, zucchini, or potatoes)
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 1/2 cup of green or black olives
  • 1 preserved lemon, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of saffron threads (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups of water or broth
  • Fresh cilantro and parsley for garnish

Preparation Method:

  1. Prepare the base: Heat olive oil in a tagine pot or heavy-bottomed pan. Add the chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until soft and fragrant.
  2. Cook the meat: Add the chicken, lamb, or beef to the pot. Sprinkle with spices (ginger, cinnamon, turmeric, paprika, black pepper, saffron, and salt). Stir to coat the meat evenly with the spices, then let it brown on all sides.
  3. Add vegetables and liquids: Arrange the vegetables around the meat in the pot. Add the chopped tomatoes, olives, and preserved lemon slices. Pour in water or broth, ensuring the ingredients are partially submerged.
  4. Simmer: Cover the tagine pot with its lid and cook on low heat for 1–2 hours, depending on the meat used. The slow-cooking process will ensure tender meat and perfectly cooked vegetables, with a rich and flavorful sauce.
  5. Final touches: Once cooked, sprinkle fresh cilantro and parsley over the dish for garnish.

Serving Method:

Tagine is traditionally served directly from the cooking vessel. Place the tagine pot at the center of the table, allowing everyone to scoop portions onto their plates. It pairs perfectly with freshly baked Moroccan bread or fluffy couscous.
