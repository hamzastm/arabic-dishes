Musakhan (Palestine)


Dish History:

Musakhan is a traditional Palestinian dish and is considered one of the main staples of Palestinian cuisine. Its history dates back to the medieval era and was originally prepared in rural areas using locally sourced ingredients. It is particularly popular in the winter and is a symbol of Palestinian hospitality.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 4 loaves of taboon bread or Arabic bread
  • 2 chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp sumac
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Method:

  1. Preparing the Chicken: Boil the chicken in water with some spices like salt and pepper until fully cooked. Remove the meat from the bones and cut it into small pieces.
  2. Preparing the Onions: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil and add the chopped onions. Sauté the onions until golden and fragrant.
  3. Preparing the Bread: Place the taboon bread or Arabic bread on a baking tray, then pour the sautéed onion mixture with olive oil over it.
  4. Adding the Chicken: Arrange the chicken pieces on top of the bread, then sprinkle sumac and drizzle lemon juice over it.
  5. Serving: Optionally, bake the dish in the oven for 10-15 minutes to enhance its flavor. Serve it warm.

Serving Method:

Musakhan is served hot with salads or yogurt.
