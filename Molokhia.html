Molokhia (Egypt)


Dish History:

Molokhia is one of the most famous dishes in Egyptian cuisine, dating back to the Pharaonic era. It is believed that molokhia was considered a royal food due to its unique taste and health benefits. It became widely prepared in Egypt during the Islamic period.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 kg of chopped molokhia (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 chickens or 1 kg of meat
  • 2 chopped onions
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Method:

  1. Prepare the broth: Boil the chicken or meat in water with salt and pepper until fully cooked. Once cooked, set the broth aside.
  2. Prepare the molokhia: In a large skillet, heat the ghee and add the chopped garlic, then stir until golden. Add the coriander and cumin, and stir again.
  3. Add the molokhia: Add the chopped molokhia to the skillet with the garlic and spices, then pour the broth over it and let it simmer for 10 minutes on low heat.

Serving Method:

Molokhia is served with white rice and pieces of meat or chicken.
