Grape Leaves (Levant)


Dish History:

Grape leaves are a popular dish in the Levant, especially in Lebanon, Syria, and Palestine. This dish is considered a symbol of Arab hospitality and has been prepared since ancient times, traditionally made for special occasions.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 kg of grape leaves
  • 1 cup of rice
  • 1/2 kg of ground meat
  • 2 chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup of toasted pine nuts
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Method:

  1. Prepare the filling: In a bowl, mix the rice with the ground meat, chopped onions, toasted pine nuts, salt, and pepper.
  2. Stuff the grape leaves: Take one grape leaf and place a portion of the filling in the center, then roll it tightly into a cylinder shape.
  3. Cook the grape leaves: Place the stuffed grape leaves in a pot covered with a layer of olive oil. Add water and lemon juice, cover the pot, and cook the grape leaves on low heat for 45 minutes until tender.

Serving Method:

Grape leaves are served with lemon juice or yogurt.
