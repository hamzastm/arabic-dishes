Koshari (Egypt)


Dish History:

Koshari is a popular Egyptian dish and one of the oldest meals consumed throughout Egypt. Its origins date back to the 19th century, where it was prepared as an affordable and nutritious meal. Over time, Koshari became one of the most famous street foods in Egypt.


    Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup black lentils
  • 1/2 cup small pasta
  • 2 chopped onions
  • 2 cups tomato puree
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas

Preparation Method:

  1. Cooking the Lentils and Rice: Wash the lentils thoroughly and cook them in boiling water until tender. Add the rice to the same pot with the lentils and cook together until the rice is fully done.
  2. Preparing the Sauce: Heat the oil in a skillet and add the chopped onions. Saut√© the onions until golden brown. Add the tomato puree and tomato paste to the onions. Stir in cumin, salt, and pepper, and let it simmer on low heat for 10 minutes.
  3. Cooking the Pasta: In another pot, boil the small pasta until cooked, then drain it.
  4. Assembling the Dish: In a serving dish, layer the rice and lentil mixture first, then add the boiled pasta, and top it with the tomato sauce.

Serving Method:

Koshari is served with spicy tomato sauce or tangy vinegar chili sauce, depending on preference.
